Design notes — not part of the site

How this concept works

Everything above is one screen-accurate homepage concept for the Divi → Kadence rebuild. Use the Preview toggle (bottom-right) to see the same system re-skinned for StatewideFloodInsurance.com — one design system, two brands.

Color tokens

Harbor navy #0A2540 — hero, footer, authority Pacific blue #0891C7 — brand cyan, made accessible Pacific bright #38C6F4 — accents on navy Sunset gold #F2A93B — CTAs only, from the logo’s sunset Paper #F7FAFC / mist #E7F0F6 grounds Statewide teal #0E8E8A — sister-brand swap

Rule

Gold appears only on things you can click to get a quote. Everything else stays cool, so the CTA is always the warmest pixel on screen.

Typography

Bricolage Grotesque (800) for display — confident and contemporary without being trendy. Public Sans (400/600) for UI and body — the U.S. government’s own typeface, a quiet nod to a FEMA/NFIP-adjacent business. Both are on Google Fonts; Kadence hosts them locally (two woff2 files, ~100 KB total).

Scale

  • H1 60/52/38 · H2 40–28 (clamped)
  • Body 16.5px / 1.65 · max 65ch
  • Labels 12px caps, +0.12em tracking

Signature motif + video

The dashed base-flood-elevation waterline — borrowed from elevation certificates — anchors the hero, the zone badge, and the CTA band. Per DECISIONS.md the raindrop video stays as the CFI hero: 720p silent loop on desktop, this static poster on mobile, data-saver, and reduced-motion, with a navy gradient guarding text contrast.

Motion

One slow 7s bob on the waterline; nothing else animates. Disabled under prefers-reduced-motion. No sliders, no carousels.
