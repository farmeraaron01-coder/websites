# CheapEarthquakeInsurance.com — What to Add (July 2026)

**Live-site baseline (verified July 13, 2026):** ~1,200 words on the homepage, clean technical setup (proper H1/H2s, Yoast robots.txt, sitemap, 2 JSON-LD blocks), CA License #0F09648 visible, "20+ years" trust claims. The site's problem is the opposite of jumpins.com's: the plumbing is fine, but there isn't enough substance for Google or AI engines to rank or cite. It reads as a quote-capture landing page, not an earthquake insurance authority.

**Strategy reminder:** this site and jumpins.com must NOT share article text. Each domain gets unique copy; they cross-link. jumpins.com's earthquake post is the consumer "do I need it / CEA vs. private" overview → this site goes **deeper and more specialized**, especially commercial (which jumpins.com barely touches).

## Priority additions (in order)

### 1. Homepage FAQ section + FAQPage schema — `content/homepage-faq.md` (30 min)
Six earthquake-specific Q&As with schema. This is the fastest GEO win on the domain: AI engines preferentially cite Q&A content, and this site currently has none.

### 2. Publish the commercial earthquake article — `content/commercial-earthquake-insurance-california.md` (1 hr)
"Commercial earthquake insurance California" is a far less crowded query than residential, the site already claims commercial expertise, and nothing on either of your sites covers it. This is the site's differentiation play — includes PML, soft-story retrofits, lender/loan covenant requirements, and business interruption angles no generic site explains.

### 3. E-E-A-T block (30 min)
- Add an "About the agency" section or page naming **Aaron Farmer** and the team, CA License #0F09648, years licensed, and the connection to Jump Insurance Services (San Diego + offices). Right now the site claims "70+ years collective experience" with zero named humans — AI engines and Google treat anonymous YMYL sites as low-trust.
- Add the same Google reviews widget used on jumpins.com (the reviews are real; show them here too). Once displayed on-page, AggregateRating can be added to this site's schema legitimately.

### 4. Cross-linking (15 min)
- jumpins.com's earthquake post already links here (verify the link is in the live post — it was missing as of July 13).
- Add reciprocal links: footer or "About" link to jumpins.com as the parent agency, and the commercial article should link to jumpins.com/business-insurance/ for non-earthquake commercial needs.

### 5. Next articles for this domain (2/month pipeline, unique to this site)
1. **"How the CEA deductible really works — with claim math"** — worked examples at 5/10/15/25%, what "deductible" means when there's no check to write, how dwelling/contents deductibles interact. (Different angle from the jumpins.com overview post.)
2. **"Soft-story and older-home earthquake insurance in California"** — pre-1980 wood frame, cripple walls, brace-and-bolt (EBB grants), how retrofits change premiums and eligibility.
3. **"Earthquake insurance for condo owners (HO-6 + master policy gaps)"** — loss assessment coverage, the massive uninsured-master-policy problem. High search intent, almost no good content exists.
4. **"Does earthquake insurance cover…?"** series page — fire following, tsunami, landslide, pool, retaining walls, foundation. Pure Q&A format with schema.

## What NOT to do
- Don't copy any paragraphs between the two domains — unique copy only, cross-linked.
- Don't add AggregateRating schema until the reviews actually display on this site's pages.
- Don't chase "cheap earthquake insurance" with thin doorway pages — the domain name already carries that query; depth is what's missing.
